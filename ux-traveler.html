Overview

Our goal was to increase the amount of meaningful information and functionalities that encourages users to book their trips through our wayfare travel app. We tested this prototype with 10 users. We aimed to make travel plans easier and faster without going to browser many apps.

Wireframe & Prototype

Understanding the User

The most meaningful way for us to learn about travel apps was to interview both present and past users.

  • Understand the customer behavior.
  • Identify the competition and difference in the industry.
  • Determine the target and audience.
  • Design, iterate and test solutions.
  • Deliver the most impactful solution within the timeframe.

Business Research

To see how Wayfare compared to competitors, we analyzed multiple travel apps.

Wayfare Advantages: Extensive travel destinations and more payment options than its competitors Travel checklist.

Wayfare Disadvantages: New to the market so it’s unknown - Low traffic and “Call to Action” buttons not big enough.


Designing Our Solution

Wireframes created to fit user insights

Lo-Fi Wireframe: We each came up with ideas to make user experience smooth and easy. We took the results of the data found in user research and compared them to our insights. We took our ideas and converted them into a paper prototype.

Mid-Fi Prototype: We tested our prototype on family and friends. Our Mid-Fi interactive prototype was used for usability tests.

Hi-Fi Prototype: Our Hi-Fi interactive prototype was tested on users. We updated the app according to the results.
